Mehndi Aftercare Guide

Immediate Aftercare

  • Let it Dry Completely: Allow the mehndi to dry naturally for at least 2–6 hours before removing it.
  • Avoid Water: Do not wash the dried mehndi with water. Instead, scrape it off gently using a butter knife or tissue.
  • Lemon-Sugar Sealant: Dabbing a lemon juice and sugar mixture on dry mehndi helps keep it in place and darken the stain.
  • Keep Hands Warm: Warmth enhances the stain. Keep your hands warm by wearing gloves or sitting near gentle heat.

Enhancing the Stain

  • Use Clove Steam: Heat cloves on a pan and expose your mehndi-stained hands to the steam for a richer color.
  • Apply Natural Oils: After scraping off the mehndi, apply mustard oil, coconut oil, or Vicks to deepen the stain.

Things to Avoid

  • ❌ No Washing for 24 Hours: Avoid washing your hands with water for at least a day.
  • ❌ No Soap or Scrubbing: Harsh soaps or rubbing can fade the stain quickly.
  • ❌ No Swimming or Excessive Moisture: Chlorinated water can weaken the stain.
